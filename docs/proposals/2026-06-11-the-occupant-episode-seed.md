# THE OCCUPANT -- episode seed (parked from SIGNAL LOST ideation)

Date: 2026-06-11
Status: parked. Disqualified from the SIGNAL LOST arc (it needs a second protagonist
and breaks the own-voice byline), but the strongest rejected pitch of the round and
worth keeping whole. Could be a standalone episode or, later, its own 2-3 chapter
adventure.

## Premise

A relay station's routing daemon logs the ultimate disposition of every distress call
it forwards: rescued, unreachable, lost. Eleven calls in thirty-one years were logged
as lost. One of those eleven callers is on the station. She reached it on a drifting
emergency capsule after her rescue never came, and she has lived in the crawlspaces
and maintenance shafts for years -- eating from emergency stores, sleeping between
hull rings, learning which hatches not to leave marks on. She does not know she is
dead in the routing log. She knows the station better than any posted technician ever
will.

## The menace (author-knowledge, never in prose)

Not supernatural. A rational, desperate human survivor who avoids contact and wins by
superior knowledge of the physical plant. She moves routing tickets, suppresses motion
sensor alerts in her territory, keeps her crawlspace heating draw indistinguishable
from resistance loss. She will not initiate violence. She has no reason to believe a
stranger is safe to approach -- being found means being returned to a system that
already decided she was gone.

## The rule

Contact is possible -- but only for someone who has read her file. She will not come
out for a stranger. She will come out for someone who knows her name and knows the
routing daemon left her to die. Approach her in a way that traps her, or that looks
like calling for extraction, and the person with eleven years of home-field advantage
stops avoiding you.

## Mechanic-level position

Nearest existing episode: vigil. vigil is about being seen; this is about not being
able to see back. The horror is being managed -- reaching a maintenance node and
finding it already adjusted, knowing something has better situational awareness of the
station than you do, and never glimpsing it.

## Gold beats to preserve (from the original pitch)

- **The handprint.** The investigating technician finds one conclusive piece of
  evidence -- a handprint in the dust on the inside of a maintenance hatch -- and
  photographs it for the incident report. The print is from the occupant's first week,
  when she was new and frightened and had not yet learned how to disappear. The
  investigator is holding the earliest surviving evidence of a person who is still
  alive, twenty feet away.
- **The retrieved note.** Early in her stay the occupant wrote a note to the posted
  technician -- clear, direct, the right ask -- left it in the operations room, and
  took it back before it was read because she could not finish the sentence. Contact
  was always possible. She chose not to. The note still exists.
- **The capsule.** Her emergency capsule sits in the secondary berth, listed as
  equipment on the rotation manifest, under a hull number that matches no active
  vessel. No one has checked it. Never shown inside.
- **Never shown:** the other ten lost callers (she knows some of their recordings by
  heart from years in the archive room); what she ate in the first two weeks.
- **Death-by-misclassification.** The investigator who files her as a potential
  hostile boarding gets a search-and-secure team, a cornered survivor, and a pressure
  incident. The classification you put on something determines how people respond to
  it.

## Shape notes

- As a standalone: the posted technician's episode -- anomalies, the dawning
  realization that the station is managed, the file, the choice of how to classify
  what she has found. Escape gate: evidence (item) + the occupant's name/file (flag);
  the good escape is cooperative contact, the deaths are misreadings.
- As an adventure: technician chapters forward or backward, with a final chapter
  played AS the occupant (arrival, the ninety days, the note). That protagonist switch
  is what disqualified it from SIGNAL LOST -- here it would be the point.
- Punishment leans standard/cruel; traces restrained (her presence is read in absences
  and small displacements -- effects, never the person).
