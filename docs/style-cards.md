# Style cards -- prose technique, distilled

Per-source craft cards for the WAKE ALONE voice. Each gives the technique to emulate, a short
illustrative line (fair-use length), and the trap to avoid. These distill the reference texts in
`corpus/` and the sources in `docs/inspiration.md`. **Emulate the method, never the wording.**

The house voice, restated: second person, present-leaning, sparse. Dread over gore. The horror is
attention -- the sense of being watched, of a wrongness that waits.

---

## The Willows -- Blackwood (1907)  ->  the unseen presence

- **Technique:** never name or describe the thing. Build it entirely from indirect signs and
  small displacements -- a sound that stops when you stop, supplies gone, a shape that is only
  almost a shape. Charge ordinary objects with dread instead of inventing a monster.
- **Cadence:** long, accreting sentences for dread that builds; then a short, flat one that lands.
- **Illustrative beat (do not copy):** the characters realize the safest thing is to stay still
  and not think, because it is their minds the presence feels.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** this is the sanity/attention mechanic. Tie loss to being noticed; make
  not-looking and not-moving viable choices. The ship is the willows.
- **Trap:** the moment you describe the creature plainly, the dread dies. Keep it inferred.

## The Yellow Wallpaper -- Gilman (1892)  ->  the eroding mind

- **Technique:** first-person-present journal whose reliability decays; the room's detail (the
  wallpaper's pattern) is re-read differently as the narrator slips. The environment seems to
  watch back.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** the model for `sanityText` -- as sanity drops, the SAME node's prose should
  re-describe the same space as subtly, then overtly, wrong. Reuse objects; change how they read.
- **POV note:** the source is first person; our house voice is second person. Convert "I see the
  bars in the pattern" into "You start to see bars in the pattern." Keep the decay, change the
  pronoun.
- **Trap:** do not explain the madness. Let the narration enact it.

## The Rime of the Ancient Mariner -- Coleridge (1798)  ->  the doomed-ship arc

- **Technique:** calamity flows from one act; the ship is becalmed and powerless; the crew die one
  by one; a lone survivor carries the guilt. Isolation -> escalation -> doom -> haunted aftermath.
- **Illustrative beat (do not copy):** day after day the ship sits with no breath, no motion --
  stuck on a dead sea.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** a clean episode spine. Open with the player's single transgression or
  failure; strip power and movement; lose the crew off-screen; gate the escape on facing the guilt.
- **Trap:** the becalming must feel like dread, not boredom -- powerlessness, not idleness.

## Lovecraft -- doctrine only (no text, no named entities)

- **Technique:** "atmosphere, not action." Build dread by subtle suggestion and selective
  associative detail; withhold; never deliver a "bald catalogue" of horrors.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** this is the "dread over gore" rule in one line. Imply; don't itemize.
- **Hard rule:** do not quote his prose at length and do not use named Mythos entities. Method only.

## Hodgson -- sea/cosmic (1907-1912)  ->  the besieged vessel

- **Technique:** ground the uncanny in authentic, physical, mechanical detail; the hull is a thin
  shell against an immense hostile dark; the threat comes from outside and tightens.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** make the ship real -- coolant, bulkheads, airlocks, the cold beyond -- so the
  wrongness has something solid to violate. The Sargasso graveyard of derelicts = a ship-graveyard
  debris field in space.
- **Trap:** atmosphere without physical grounding floats; name the real systems.

## Mary Celeste (1872, real) + Hurley's *Endurance* photos  ->  absence and the indifferent void

- **Technique:** the dread of the empty-but-recently-occupied scene -- belongings undisturbed, a
  meal half-eaten, no bodies, no note. And the polar-isolation image: a tiny crewed thing in an
  immense, uncaring white/black.
- **For WAKE ALONE:** open many episodes on absence, not threat. The crew is simply gone. Let the
  player furnish the horror.
- **Trap:** don't over-explain where everyone went. The unanswered absence is the point.
