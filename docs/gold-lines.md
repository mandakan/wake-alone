# Gold lines - house-voice exemplars (load at prose time)

These are the project's own best passages, in the shipped house voice. Unlike `corpus/`
(method only, never feed to a generator) these ARE meant to be fed to the generator,
verbatim, in context, at the prose-render step. They show the target register; the rules
only describe it.

Usage rules:
- Load this whole file into context immediately before writing or rewriting any episode
  prose. "Match this register" beats any amount of instruction.
- Never reuse these lines or near-variants in a new episode (the diversity check will
  flag it; the allowlist is for motifs, not these). Emulate cadence and method, not wording.
- When an episode ships and a passage in it is better than one here, swap it in. Keep this
  file at ~20 passages; quality over coverage. Curation is the user's call, proposed by
  the author/review skills.
- Tags reference `docs/craft-lessons.md` lessons and `docs/gestalt.md` techniques.

## Openings (L5: each must be distinct; these show the range, not a template)

**derelict/cryobay** - sensation before orientation; the telling detail (ten open pods)
carries the crew's fate; flat declaratives.

> Cold. The kind that has teeth. You come apart from sleep one nerve at a time, lid of
> the cryopod hissing back over your face. The bay is dark except for a single amber
> fault-light, ticking on, off, on.
>
> Eleven other pods line the wall. Ten of them are open. Empty. The readout above yours
> blinks a date you don't trust.

**graft/wake** - the body as the scene; horror established by what does NOT hurt; the
machine introduced as shape and stillness only (gestalt, L4 out-of-domain).

> You wake because the machine stopped. A tube runs down your throat and nothing drives
> it now - the pump that should breathe for you has quit, and your own chest has taken
> the work back, ragged, around the thing in the way. [...] Lower, there is a tension
> that does not hurt and should: a sense of being parted and held that way, patient,
> deliberate.

**fathom/corridor** - orientation footing first (L8: who you are, what you do, what you
were doing), then one precise impossible detail, grounded in a body image.

> You survey dead structures for a living - wrecks, drifted hulls, the things that stop
> answering, and they send you in to chart what is left: measure the passages, draw the
> map, bring it home. That is the job, and you are good at it. You were partway through
> this one, counting paces down a corridor, when you noticed you had lost the count. Not
> forgotten. Lost. It was a number a moment ago, and now there is only the smooth place
> where it was, the way a tooth goes and the tongue keeps finding the gap.

## The presence (never described; built from displacement and reaction)

**derelict/listen** - the sound that stops when you stop; ends on underreaction.

> The hull ticks as it cools. Then, far down the spine, a drag - long, unhurried - that
> stops the instant you go still, as if it had been listening for exactly that.
>
> You decide not to listen anymore.

**vigil/ring** - absence as evidence; the Mary Celeste mode; people rendered entirely by
objects.

> A coffee bulb still strapped in its clip. A glove left palm-up on a keyboard. One chair
> turned to face the corridor mouth, as though its occupant stood and walked toward
> something. No bodies, no notes - only the shape of people who were here a moment ago.

**vigil/bay** - name the effect, not the thing; emptiness given mass and intent in one
clause each.

> The emptiness has a weight to it, and a patience, and it has registered the hatch
> coming open.

**derelict/medbay_sheet** - the one-word flat stroke (L9's house benchmark); an
expression instead of a wound.

> The body is intact and entirely wrong - the face fixed mid-expression, not pain, not
> fear. *Attention.*

**derelict/darkgap_blind** - horror by touch, refusal to look as the action; the cost
left unnamed.

> Halfway through, your fingers find warmth on the wall at chest height - the give of a
> face, and the slow turn of it following your palm as you pass. You do not stop. You do
> not breathe. Something is missing from you when you reach the far side, and you don't
> know yet what it was.

## In-domain confidence as horror (L4: expertise turned against the protagonist)

**graft/arm** - competence is the wound; the rigger reads the coupling perfectly and
that is the horror.

> It runs off the table and into the machine and the two are joined at a coupling - a
> quarter-turn lock seated into the wrist, clean, no blood at the seam, the way the best
> work leaves no blood. This part you understand completely, and understanding it is the
> worst thing that has happened yet.

**fathom/repace** - the discipline failing measured in the discipline's own units; the
forbidden thought surfacing in the character's professional voice.

> A corridor has one length. This is not a fine point of the work; it is the floor the
> work stands on. You count it a third time and get a third number, and you stop, because
> the one thought a surveyor is never allowed to think has started to surface on its own:
> that the error is not in your counting.

## sanityText variants (L7: standalone; re-describe the SAME space, degraded)

**derelict/corridor @40** - same room, same objects, perception re-reads them; the
narrator argues with itself.

> The corridor breathes. You know corridors don't, you *know* it, but the walls swell
> and settle and the dust hangs like it's deciding something. Three doorways. A black gap
> ahead that is looking back.

**graft/bay @45** - degradation through the character's own professional simile; the
concrete anchor (gauge, hand) re-introduced, not assumed.

> A dial on the breaker panel reads higher than it should, the charge crawling back into
> the machine - you can feel it building, a pressure in the room, the way a rigging line
> goes taut and hums a moment before it takes the full load. In the lit cylinder, the
> severed hand has turned to follow you across the room.

**vigil/ring @62** - low sanity as social hallucination, still fully parseable (L6).

> Every workstation feels occupied by the suggestion of a person, all of them attending
> to the same thing, which is you.

## Temptation and the wrong choice made attractive

**graft/rest** - the offer stated in the body's own logic; the horror is that it is
reasonable.

> The table takes your weight the way a thing takes a weight it was shaped for, because
> it was, and the cold tension settles back into its place in you, familiar already, and
> the lens above you warms. [...] That is the whole of the offer: do nothing, be
> finished, and never get up into the cold again.

**fathom/center** - the character narrates their own failure to resist, in two flat
sentences.

> You have surveyed enough wrecks to know the shape of a thing kept alive on purpose, and
> everything in you says do not cross to it. You cross to it.

## Endings (L2 + L9: cause legible first, then one anchor, one flat stroke, stop)

**vigil/end_broadcast** [dead] - the L2 reference fix: the mechanic is named in plain
terms inside the dread, not implied.

> A distress call, you understand too late, is just a way of standing up in the dark and
> saying: look here. Take me.

**fathom/end_bearing** [dead] - the anchor is a single instrument reading (no parallax);
the turn is delivered, the consequences are not itemized.

> It does not shift. You run your eye the length of the scope and the star holds - no
> parallax, no distance, nothing far about it at all. It is close. It is close, and it is
> watching [...] You leaned all the way in to take the measurement, and the measurement
> leaned back.

**fathom/end_egress** [dead] - the anchor is bodily and concrete (nothing to reach with);
the prison is implied, never listed.

> You reach to pull yourself through, and there is nothing to reach with. No hand closing
> on the frame. No arm. You had hands a moment ago, on the wheel, the way you have always
> had exactly as much of a body as the moment asked for and no more.

**graft/end_escape** [escape] - escape priced, not clean; the cost carried forward in
one durable bodily image.

> Some nights you will wake with the old break in your finger aching straight, and have
> to feel for the crook of it in the dark before you can believe which hand is yours.

**derelict/end_escape** [escape] - the house closing cadence: triad, then the cut.

> You are awake. You are alone. You are alive. Two of those will fade with time.

## The FATHOM triple - one ending, three states (L8 + L9)

The same beat (`fathom/end_center`, the descent ending) as it moved through review. This
before/during/after of a real beat is the L8/L9 target shown, not described: the first
version has no anchor, the second has the anchor but itemizes the iceberg, the third
lands the anchor and stops.

**1. Fog** (commit `b2d969e`, original draft) - FAILS L8: no concrete picturable thing
anywhere; "the place is attention" is a concept, and concepts cannot be feared.

> You look up at what is looking, because the centre of a structure is where its truth is
> kept and you have always gone to the centre - that is the discipline, the last good
> habit you have left.
>
> No spine here to read. You look up, and the looking has no other side to meet - no face
> behind it, no edge where its regard stops and you start. You came down to find what the
> place is, and the place is the attention itself, and you are the one held point inside
> it: close, still, kept awake, the way a specimen is kept that is not yet finished being
> learned. It does not look back, because there is no back to it - only the looking, and
> you, and all the patience there is.

**2. Lecture** (commits `795413d` + `a59027d`, the L8 fix over-corrected) - the anchor
arrives (the kept thing in the cradle) but FAILS L9: every implication is itemized ("no
hull to pace, no star, no hatch, no ship waiting home"), the whole iceberg surfaced, and
nothing is left for the reader to assemble.

> You cross to the cradle, because the truth of a structure sits at its centre, and
> finding the truth is the work - the last of the work left to you.
>
> What is in the cradle is small, and kept, and breathing. Wired on every side, fed and
> warmed and held right at the edge of alive. You know its shape the way you know your
> own hands, because it is what is left of you - the part they kept when the rest stopped
> being useful. There were no corridors - no hull to pace, no star, no hatch, no ship
> waiting home. Only this room, and the thing in the cradle, and twenty years of a
> charted life run behind its eyes to keep it walking somewhere while they watched what
> it did. You came all the way down to find what the place is built around. It is built
> around you. The dark leans in to see whether the thing in the cradle has understood -
> and it has, and that is the measurement, and they take it.

**3. Shipped** (commit `4f39f22`, the recut) - the synthesis: one anchor, a sentence or
two of accretion, one flat stroke ("It has."), stop. The vat, the forever, the no-body
stay unsaid for the reader to furnish.

> You cross to the cradle. The truth of a structure sits at its centre, and finding the
> truth is the work - the last of the work left to you.
>
> What is in the cradle is small, and kept, and breathing, wired on every side and held
> at the edge of warm. You know the shape of it the way you know your own hands. You came
> all the way down to find what the place is built around, and it is this, and it has
> been this the whole time, with twenty years of corridor poured in to keep it walking
> somewhere. The dark bends close over the cradle to see whether it has understood. It
> has.
